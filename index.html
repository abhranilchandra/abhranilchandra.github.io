I’m a 1st year PhD student in Computer Science at UMass Amherst (advised by Scott Niekum). I recently finished my Research Masters in CS from the University of Waterloo (advisor Prof. Sebastian Fischmeister), working at MILA with Dr. Rishabh Agarwal, Prof. Aaron Courville and at DeepMind with Dr. Sherry Yang, and Dr. Bo Dai for my masters thesis. My research focuses on building Foundation-Model Agents for Sequential Decision-Making—scalable, general-purpose agents that learn, adapt, explore, reason, plan, act, and self-improve.

Selected Research

Shape of Thought (SoT): When Distribution Can Matter More than Correctness
ICLR 2025 under review

We show LMs can improve reasoning from entirely incorrect CoTs when the training distribution matches model-generated traces, suggesting distributional alignment can trump final-answer correctness.

paper
VideoAgent: Self-Improving Generative World Model
TMLR 2025 · RLBrew Spotlight

A framework that iteratively refines video plans using VLM-guided feedback and self-consistency, yielding up to 4× improvement in task success.

paper
ReFeR: Hierarchical Agents for Reasoning & Evaluation

A hierarchical framework with small LLM “reviewers” and large LLM “area chairs” for scalable oversight.
